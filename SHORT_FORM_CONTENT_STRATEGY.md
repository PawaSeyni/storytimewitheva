Distinct workstream from the site build. Goal: produce short-form video content across YouTube Shorts, TikTok, and Instagram Reels (and potentially Threads for text amplification) that drives traffic to storytimewitheva.com, where the lead-capture funnel takes over.

**Status:** Not started. This document is a starter, not a finished plan.
**Owner:** Eva Gallo
**Last updated:** 2026-05-21

---

## Why this is separate from the site build

The site build (gates A/B/C, see `PUNCH_LIST.md` and `RUNBOOK.md`) is **steady-state operations**:
- Hosting, forms, automation, analytics — all running, just needs maintenance.

Short-form content is **a creative production pipeline**:
- Different tooling (CapCut / Final Cut / Premiere Rush / phone editor)
- Different cadence (multiple posts/week, ongoing)
- Different success metrics (views, watch time, follower growth, click-throughs to site)
- Different monetization paths (ad revenue, sponsorships, affiliate, product sales)

Treating them together would muddle both. This document tracks the content strategy independently.

---

## North star

**Drive 100+ engaged email signups per month** to `storytimewitheva.com` via short-form content within 6 months of consistent posting.

The site already converts signups (lead-capture funnel is live and tested). The constraint is **awareness + traffic**. Short-form is the cheapest, fastest way to build both for a children's-book imprint with strong visual + audio storytelling assets.

## Channels under consideration

| Channel | Format | Why it might fit | Why it might not |
|---|---|---|---|
| **YouTube Shorts + main channel** | 30-60s vertical videos + occasional 5-15 min read-alouds | YouTube favors educational/family content. Long-form read-alouds have huge evergreen search value. Discoverable for years via search. | Algorithm rewards consistency. Slow growth initially. |
| **TikTok** | 15-60s vertical | Fastest organic discovery. Strong "MomTok" / "BookTok" communities. Algorithm-friendly to small accounts. | Trending audio dependency; less evergreen. US ban risk lingers. |
| **Instagram Reels** | 15-60s vertical | Eva already has Instagram (`@galloeva2612`). Cross-posts well from TikTok. Shopping/affiliate features. | Reach has declined; harder to grow without paid boost. |
| **Threads** | Text + image | Low effort amplification of any video post. Anthropic-aligned creators are doing well there. | Limited video reach; mostly text. |
| **Pinterest video pins** | 6-15s vertical | Pinterest is searchable, evergreen. Parent audience strong. Existing pin assets in `/Eva/Online Strategy/`. | Slow growth, less viral mechanism. |

**Recommended starting concentration:**
- **Pick TWO channels max** to start. Each one is real production work.
- Best starter pair: **TikTok + YouTube Shorts** — same vertical aspect ratio, same content can post to both. Threads for text amplification of links.
- Add Instagram Reels later as a cross-post (zero marginal effort if content is already vertical).

## Content pillars (brainstorm — not committed)

For a children's book imprint, ideas worth testing:

1. **Read-alouds** — Eva reading a spread or full short book, with the illustrations on screen. Evergreen, very searchable.
2. **Bilingual moments** — same scene read in EN / ES / FR. Trilingual hook is unique.
3. **Behind-the-scenes** — illustration in progress, book editing, KDP setup. Authors / writers love this; great LinkedIn cross-post.
4. **Parent tips** — "3 ways to make reading time stick" / "What to do when kids refuse books in Spanish". Pull from the actual content of the lead magnets (Parents Guide, Bedtime Chart).
5. **Activity demos** — print a flashcard, set up the bingo board, do a craft. Drives both the activity demos on the site AND the PDF downloads.
6. **Storytime ASMR / bedtime sleep stories** — calming voice over book illustrations + soft music. Huge sleep-content niche.
7. **Author POV** — Eva's perspective on writing for kids, multicultural representation, dual identity. Memoir-adjacent.
8. **Quick book recommendations** — "If your kid loves X, try Y." Plugs Eva's books + adjacent titles. Builds taste-maker authority.

**Most differentiated:** trilingual content (#2). Few children's authors do EN/ES/FR. The bilingual starter kit and lead magnets already lean into this — the content should match.

## Monetization layers (long term)

The site is the conversion funnel. Short-form is the traffic engine. Money flows from:

1. **Direct book sales** (Amazon KDP affiliate + author royalties) — already wired, BookCard links drive clicks
2. **Email list growth** → eventually a paid product (e.g., the Eva Gallo Multilingual Reading Kit, $19) sold to the warmed list
3. **YouTube ad revenue** (once channel hits 1K subs + 4K watch hours for monetization eligibility)
4. **TikTok Creator Rewards** (once channel hits 10K followers)
5. **Brand sponsorships** — children's brands, language-learning apps, parenting products (only after audience is established)
6. **School visits / speaking** — Eva's reputation as a children's author can grow through visibility
7. **Patreon / membership tier** — for hardcore fans wanting early access to new books, behind-the-scenes content

**Single biggest leverage point: list growth.** Every product launch in 2-5 years will be sent first to the email list. Short-form's job is to feed the list.
